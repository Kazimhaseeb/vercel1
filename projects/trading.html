BOT 01 / ALPACA MARKETS

Alpaca Crypto Bot

An advanced multi-symbol equity/crypto trading bot built on Alpaca Markets with SMA crossover signals, RSI confirmation, dynamic position sizing, and a full suite of risk controls including cooldowns, consecutive-loss protection, take-profit, and trailing stop-losses.

SMA Crossover RSI Filter Take Profit Trailing Stop Loss Protection Paper & Live Multi-Symbol

This is arguably the most feature-complete bot in the portfolio. It targets multiple equities simultaneously (e.g., NVDA, AAPL, TSLA) using minute-level bars and combines a Simple Moving Average (SMA) crossover for trend direction with an RSI momentum filter to avoid entering overbought or oversold conditions. What sets it apart is its TradeTracker system — a persistent trade-history logger that enforces cooldown periods after exits and halts new entries after a configurable number of consecutive losses. Position sizing can be either fixed-dollar or a dynamic percentage of account equity, making it appropriate for both small and large accounts.

How the Strategy Works

1
Market Hours Check
The bot verifies NYSE market hours (9:30 AM – 4:00 PM ET, weekdays only) before every cycle. If the market is closed, it sleeps and waits.
2
Cooldown & Loss Protection
Before evaluating any symbol, the TradeTracker checks if the symbol is under cooldown (15 minutes post-exit) or if it has hit the maximum consecutive losses limit (2). If blocked, the symbol is skipped for new entries but still managed if a position is open.
3
Fetch OHLCV Data & Calculate Indicators
Historical bar data is fetched from Alpaca's API. Two SMAs (SMA_X and SMA_Y, configurable periods) and RSI (default period 14) are calculated on the close price series.
4
Signal Generation
A BUY signal fires when the close price crosses above SMA_X on the most recent bar. A SELL signal fires when the close crosses below SMA_Y. RSI acts as a confirmation filter: buys are blocked if RSI > 70 (overbought), sells are blocked if RSI < 30 (oversold).
5
Take Profit Check
For any open long position, the bot checks if the current unrealised P&L has reached the configurable take-profit threshold (5%). If yes, the position is closed immediately via a market close order.
6
Dynamic Position Sizing
Trade size is computed as either a fixed dollar amount or a percentage of current account equity (3% default). This ensures the bot naturally scales with account growth or drawdown.
7
Order Execution & Trade Logging
Buy orders are placed as market orders. Sell exits use trailing stop orders (trail = stop-loss %). On every iteration, the bot detects positions that disappeared (closed externally or via stop) and logs the P&L to persist the trade history for future cooldown/loss checks.

Risk Management Framework

Trailing Stop-Loss: Sells use a trailing stop at 0.3% trail, locking in profits as price advances.
Cooldown Protection: 15-minute mandatory cooldown after every trade exit prevents revenge trading.
Consecutive Loss Guard: Trading halted for a symbol after 2 consecutive losses. Auto-resets on a win.
Take Profit: Positions closed at +5% unrealised profit to secure gains before reversal.
Max Active Trades: Hard cap of 2 simultaneous positions across all symbols.
Per-Stock Limit: Maximum of 1 open trade per symbol at any time.

BOT 02 / ALPACA MARKETS

Alpaca RSI Bot

A streamlined, clean implementation of a dual-SMA crossover strategy with an RSI momentum filter, supporting multiple symbols with configurable active trade and per-stock limits. Designed as a reliable, no-frills equity trading engine for Alpaca Markets.

SMA Crossover RSI Filter Trailing Stop Short Selling Multi-Symbol

The Alpaca RSI Bot is the clean-room core version of the strategy, stripped of advanced features like the TradeTracker and dynamic position sizing, making it easier to read, audit, and extend. It shares the same SMA crossover logic and RSI filter but uses a fixed notional trade amount. It also supports short-selling when enabled, and exits long positions via trailing stop on a sell signal. This is the ideal starting point or template for customisation.

Strategy Overview

The bot fetches OHLCV bar data for each symbol from Alpaca and calculates two SMAs (SMA_X for buy threshold and SMA_Y for sell threshold) along with RSI. A buy signal occurs when price crosses above SMA_X; a sell signal occurs when price crosses below SMA_Y. The RSI filter prevents buying into overbought conditions (RSI > 70) and prevents shorting into oversold conditions (RSI < 30).

On a sell signal with an open long position, rather than placing a plain sell, the bot places a trailing stop order to protect profits while giving the position room to continue if the trend resumes. Short selling is independently configurable — when enabled, sell signals without an open position will open a short.

Entry Conditions

  • RSI must be below 70 (not overbought)
  • Active trades below MAX_ACTIVE_TRADES
  • Per-stock count below max_trades_per_stock

Exit Conditions

  • Price crosses below SMA_Y (sell signal)
  • Exit via trailing stop order
  • RSI below 30 blocks short entries
  • Market hours: 9:30 AM – 4:00 PM ET

BOT 03 / ALPACA MARKETS

Alpaca WebSocket Bot

A real-time, event-driven trading bot that subscribes to Alpaca's WebSocket stream for live bar data. Implements a dip-buying strategy with automatic profit-threshold trailing stop activation and trade history logging to Excel.

WebSocket Streaming Dip Buy Trailing Stop Auto-Reconnect Excel Logging

Unlike poll-based bots, this bot connects to Alpaca's IEX WebSocket stream (wss://stream.data.alpaca.markets/v2/iex) and reacts to bar events in real time. The strategy is elegantly simple: buy when price drops a configurable percentage below the last trade price (dip-buying), then activate a trailing stop once the position has reached a minimum profit threshold. All trade activity is logged and exported to an Excel spreadsheet for review. The WebSocket connection includes exponential backoff reconnection logic to handle network interruptions gracefully.

Strategy Logic

1
Connect to WebSocket Stream
On startup, authenticates to Alpaca's data stream and subscribes to 1-minute bar updates for the configured symbol.
2
Dip Detection — Buy Signal
Every bar update is compared to the last trade fill price. If the current close is at or below last_price × (1 − BUY_DROP_PERCENT), a market buy is placed.
3
Profit Threshold — Trailing Stop Activation
If the current price is at or above last_price × (1 + PROFIT_THRESHOLD), a trailing stop sell order is placed to capture gains while allowing further upside.
4
Auto-Reconnect on Disconnect
On WebSocket close, the bot waits with exponential backoff (starting at 3 seconds, doubling up to 60 seconds) before reconnecting — but only within trading hours.
5
Excel Trade Log Export
Every buy and sell event is appended to a trade_history.xlsx file. If the file already exists, new rows are merged, maintaining a complete history.
Extended Trading Hours

This bot supports pre-market (4:00 AM) through after-hours (8:00 PM ET) trading, giving it an edge for reacting to overnight news and earnings events — broader coverage than standard 9:30–4:00 PM sessions.

BOT 04 / BINANCE

Binance New Listing Bot

A market-discovery bot that continuously monitors Binance for newly listed trading pairs and automatically enters a position when a new coin is detected, targeting a 2× price target with a configurable stop-loss.

New Listing Auto-Discovery 2× Target Stop Loss Spot Market

New coin listings on Binance are notorious for explosive price action in the first minutes and hours. This bot exploits that phenomenon by maintaining a snapshot of all known Binance symbols and polling periodically for additions. When a new pair matching the configured quote asset (e.g., USDT) appears, it buys immediately at market price, then enters a monitoring loop watching for either a 2× price target or the configured stop-loss level. A persistent JSON file stores the known coin set across restarts so no listing is missed.

Strategy Logic

On first run, the bot fetches all current Binance tickers and saves them as the "seen" baseline. On every subsequent poll interval, it fetches all current tickers again and computes the difference (new coins = current − seen). Any new symbol matching the configured quote asset is immediately purchased at market price for the configured spend amount.

After purchasing, the bot tracks the coin in a dedicated monitoring loop, checking price every 10 seconds against the 2× target and stop-loss price. The moment either threshold is hit, a full market sell of the balance is placed. Trade logs are written to a local text file for audit purposes.

Entry Logic

  • Binance symbol not in known set
  • Symbol ends with configured quote asset
  • Buy at market for fixed spend amount
  • Record buy price from fill data

Exit Logic

  • Price ≥ 2× entry price → full market sell
  • Price ≤ stop-loss → full market sell
  • Sell full available balance of base asset
  • Log both buy and sell events
Testnet Ready

The bot is configured to point to the Binance testnet (testnet.binance.vision) by default, allowing safe strategy testing without real funds.

Binance RSI Futures Bot

Features a tiered take-profit system and a trailing stop-loss.

RSI + S/R Levels Top 500 Coins Short Selling 3-Level TP CoinGecko Integration Futures Market

This is one of the most ambitious bots in the portfolio. It begins by fetching the top 500 cryptocurrencies by market capitalisation from CoinGecko, then cross-references against all available Binance Futures symbols to build a filtered list of actionable USDT-margined perpetual contracts. For each symbol, it fetches 5-minute OHLCV data, calculates RSI(14), and identifies rolling 20-bar support and resistance levels. A LONG signal fires when RSI ≤ 30 and price is at or below support; a SHORT signal fires when RSI ≥ 70 and price is at or above resistance.

Multi-Level Take Profit System

When a trade is entered, three take-profit levels are set relative to the entry price. For a LONG trade: TP1 at +3%, TP2 at +6%, TP3 at +9%. For a SHORT: TP1 at −3%, TP2 at −6%, TP3 at −9%. At each TP level, a partial exit is taken (25%, 50%, 25% of position size respectively). After TP1 is hit, the stop-loss is moved to the TP1 level, creating a risk-free position going forward. This progressively locks in profits while keeping exposure for larger moves.

CoinGecko Market Cap Filtering

Rather than trading obscure low-liquidity coins, the bot only considers assets that rank in the top 500 by market capitalisation on CoinGecko. This ensures that even under high-frequency scanning, all traded coins have reasonable liquidity and genuine market activity. The CoinGecko API is queried in paginated batches of 250 coins with rate-limit handling.

Interactive Confirmation

When a trade signal is detected, the bot prompts the user with the symbol, direction, and entry price before placing the order. This safety check prevents unintended automated trades and keeps the operator in control of every execution.

15-Minute Trend Confirmation (Higher Timeframe)

The 15m chart acts as the gatekeeper. Three indicators must confirm trend direction before the bot will look for 1m entries:

Supertrend: A volatility-adjusted trend indicator that switches between bullish and bearish signals based on ATR bands. Must align with trade direction.
MACD Histogram: The MACD histogram must be positive for longs and negative for shorts, confirming momentum direction on the higher timeframe.
EMA Crossover (20/50): The fast EMA (20) must be above the slow EMA (50) for bullish bias, and below for bearish bias.

1-Minute Entry Triggers (Lower Timeframe)

Once the 15m confirms the macro bias, the bot scans the 1m chart for precision entry signals:

MACD Crossover: A bullish MACD crossover on the 1m for long entries, bearish crossover for shorts.
StochRSI Exit: StochRSI exiting oversold territory (crossing above 20) for longs; exiting overbought (crossing below 80) for shorts.
RSI Bounce: RSI bouncing from key levels (e.g., 30–40 for longs) confirms momentum alignment before entry.

Risk Management

ATR-Based Stop Loss: The stop-loss distance is calculated dynamically using the Average True Range (ATR × 1.5 multiplier). This adapts the stop to current market volatility — wider stops in volatile conditions, tighter in quiet markets.

Equity-Based Position Sizing: Each trade risks a configurable fixed percentage of account balance (RISK_PER_TRADE parameter), ensuring consistent risk regardless of account size or price level.

Streamlit Dashboard Features

Backtesting Tab: Enter any symbol, click "Run Backtest" — results include equity curve chart, trade P&L chart, and a full trade log table.
Live Bot Control: Start/Stop the bot with a single button click. Real-time log output streams directly to the dashboard.
Market Analysis Panel: Live indicator readings, current trend bias, and engine status displayed once bot is running.
Safety First: Defaults to Binance Testnet. Live trading requires an explicit environment variable override, preventing accidental live orders.

BOT 07 / BYBIT

Bybit Grid Trading Bot

A dynamic grid trading engine for Bybit's Unified Trading API (linear perpetuals). Generates a configurable grid of price levels around the entry price and places limit orders at each level as the price crosses grid boundaries, enabling systematic profit capture in ranging or trending markets.

Grid Trading Limit Orders Configurable Leverage TP & SL Per Order Linear Perpetuals Testnet & Live

Grid trading is a market-neutral strategy that places buy and sell limit orders at regular price intervals above and below the current price. This Bybit implementation generates a dynamic grid around the bot's startup price using configurable range and level count parameters. As the price moves through the grid, the bot detects crossings and places new limit orders with individual stop-loss and take-profit targets. The strategy profits from back-and-forth price oscillation — common in sideways or low-volatility markets.

Grid Generation Algorithm

At startup, the bot fetches the current market price as the "start price." From there, it generates an equal number of grid levels above and below the mid-price. The step size between levels is computed as:

step_size = (GRID_RANGE_MAX - GRID_RANGE_MIN) / (GRID_LEVELS - 1) upper_grid = [mid * (1 + (GRID_RANGE_MIN + i * step) / 100) for i in range(5)] lower_grid = [mid * (1 - (GRID_RANGE_MIN + i * step) / 100) for i in range(5)] grid = sorted(upper_grid + lower_grid)

The resulting 10 levels span symmetrically around the current price, creating buy zones below and sell zones above.

Order Placement Logic

In the main monitoring loop (5-second poll), the bot compares current price to the previous cycle's price. When the price crosses a grid level upward (previous price < grid level ≤ current price), a LONG limit order is placed at that level. When price crosses downward, a SHORT limit order is placed. Each order includes individual TP and SL prices calculated from configurable percentages.

Upward Crossing

  • Price crosses grid level from below
  • Place BUY limit at grid price
  • TP = grid_price × (1 + TP_PERCENT)
  • SL = grid_price × (1 − SL_PERCENT)

Downward Crossing

  • Price crosses grid level from above
  • Place SELL limit at grid price
  • TP = grid_price × (1 − TP_PERCENT)
  • SL = grid_price × (1 + SL_PERCENT)

BOT 08 / IG BROKER

IG Broker Five-Indicator Bot

A sophisticated CFD/forex trading bot for IG Markets. Uses five independent technical strategies — RSI, EMA crossover, Bollinger Band breakout, Stochastic oscillator reversal, and Bollinger Band bounce — and generates a trade signal only when at least 3 of 5 strategies agree, creating a high-confidence consensus-based entry system.

5-Indicator Consensus RSI EMA Crossover Bollinger Bands Stochastic IG Markets API

This bot connects to IG Markets via the trading_ig Python library and trades any instrument via its EPIC identifier (e.g., Forex, Indices, Commodities). The defining feature is its consensus-based signal system: five distinct technical strategies each vote independently, and a trade is only executed when the majority (≥ 3 of 5) agree on direction. This dramatically reduces false signals and noise while maintaining responsiveness to genuine market moves. Historical data is cached daily to a CSV file for efficiency.

The Five Strategies Explained

1. Momentum (RSI): Generates a BUY vote when RSI(14) < 30 (oversold) and a SELL vote when RSI > 70 (overbought). Classic momentum reversal.
2. Moving Average Crossover (EMA-20): BUY when price is above EMA(20); SELL when price is below. Trend-following component.
3. Breakout (Bollinger Bands): BUY when close breaks above the upper Bollinger Band; SELL when it breaks below the lower band. Momentum breakout strategy.
4. Reversal (Stochastic Oscillator): BUY when Stochastic(14,3) < 20 (oversold); SELL when Stochastic > 80 (overbought). Mean-reversion component.
5. Bollinger Band Bounce: BUY when price touches or crosses below the lower band then bounces back above it; SELL on bounce from upper band. Precise reversal signal.
Consensus Logic

Each strategy independently computes a boolean vote (True/False) for buy and sell direction. These five votes are summed: if the sum ≥ 3, the corresponding signal is fired. This ensures no single noisy indicator can cause a false entry — the market must be genuinely aligned across multiple analytical frameworks.

Data Caching System

Historical price data (210 data points) is fetched from IG Markets and cached to a daily CSV file named historicalData_YYYY-MM-DD.csv. On subsequent runs within the same day, the bot reads from the cache instead of making a live API call, reducing API usage and improving startup speed. The current market price is always fetched live to append to the historical dataset for indicator calculation.

BOT 09 / TASTYTRADE

TastyTrade Options Bot

An advanced options trading system for TastyTrade, implementing an Iron Condor / Credit Spread strategy on SPX (S&P 500 Index) options. Streams real-time option quotes via DXLink, identifies optimal ATM credit spreads by maximising extrinsic value, and automatically places 0-DTE (zero days to expiration) iron condor orders at a configured execution time.

Iron Condor Credit Spreads SPX Options 0-DTE Strategy DXLink Streaming Extrinsic Optimiser

This is the most architecturally complex bot in the portfolio, involving multiple cooperating Python modules and asynchronous programming (asyncio). The strategy targets 0-DTE SPX options — options expiring the same day they are traded — using an iron condor structure (short call spread + short put spread). The bot streams live option chain quotes via TastyTrade's DXLink WebSocket API, computes extrinsic value for all near-the-money strikes, identifies the optimal short/long combination for both the call and put spreads, and places orders at a pre-configured execution time each day.

Architecture — Module Breakdown

OptionsExchange (Abstract Base): Defines the interface for any options exchange connection — methods for option chain retrieval, ATM option selection, quote fetching, underlying price, and order placement.
TastyTrade (Implementation): Concrete implementation of OptionsExchange for the TastyTrade API. Handles DXLink WebSocket quote streaming, ATM option selection (3 strikes above/below current price), and order placement for credit spread legs.
OptimalTrade (Strategy Engine): The core strategy logic. Computes extrinsic value for each option, finds the highest-extrinsic short leg within ±$5 of ATM, and pairs it with a $10-wide protection leg. Displays a live options table in the terminal.
LivePrices (Price Streaming): Handles real-time underlying price subscriptions via DXLink for accurate Greeks and moneyness calculations.
bot.py (Backtest Engine): Simulates the iron condor strategy against historical option data, calculating daily P&L for put and call spreads, logging results to CSV.

Extrinsic Value Optimisation

The strategy's edge comes from collecting extrinsic (time) value at the optimal strike. For each near-ATM option, the bot calculates:

# For a CALL option: intrinsic = max(0, current_price - strike_price) extrinsic = max(0, bid_price - intrinsic) # For a PUT option: intrinsic = max(0, strike_price - current_price) extrinsic = max(0, bid_price - intrinsic)

The short leg is chosen as the strike with the highest extrinsic value within ±$5 of ATM. This ensures maximum premium collection while staying near the money.
